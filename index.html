Dundalk cinema viability study

A demographic study using GIS network analysis methods

In 2014, I studied the viability of two proposed cinemas in Dundalk, Ireland, as well as the impact their construction would have on the potential audiences for other cinemas in the Louth/Meath/Cavan area, using ArcGIS's Network Analyst tool.

Mapping language death in the Omeath Gaeltacht

A geolinguistic study using GIS and census data [Not yet uploaded]

A study, completed in 2013, into the geographic trends of the death of the Irish language in Omeath in early 20th century. Drawing on Nancy Dorian's work on intergenerational transmission of Scots Gaelic, I used ArcGIS and government census records to trace the demographic and linguistic shifts in the historic Omeath Gaeltacht.

Graphing the Vatican's canonizations from 1800-2016

Made using the Plotly package for R

Recently, in the process of learning how to use R for GIS, I plotted the last 200 years of canonizations by the Holy See, with a local regression, visually demonstrating the massive increase in canonizations since John Paul II.

A geographic history of Dublin's cinemas

From the 1950s to the present [Not yet uploaded]

While working at GAMMA, I designed a series of maps plotting the changes in the geography of Dublin's cinemas from the 1950s to the present. This required tracking down hard-to-find information about the administrative geography of Dublin in the 1950s, and the locations of now-defunct cinemas.
